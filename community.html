Why the Program Exists

In January 2022, an underwater volcano erupted near Tonga in the Pacific. It caused a tsunami that devastated infrastructure and severely impacted the livelihoods of 85 percent of the island’s population. The disaster caused damage and losses of at least US$90.4 million, or 18.5 percent of Tonga’s GDP. The Fiscal Resilience Program is built around a practical question: how can countries like Tonga protect their economies from this kind of shock?

Program Story

The program starts from the real fiscal consequences of disasters in Pacific island economies.
    Fiscal resilience for Pacific SIDS is built before the next shock — through shared learning, stronger buffers, and public finance systems that protect communities when they need them most.
