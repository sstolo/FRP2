Buffers

Introduce explicit fiscal buffer floors linked to disaster and shock exposure. For Pacific SIDS, buffers are not only a macroeconomic safeguard; they are a practical tool for keeping essential services running after climate, disaster, or external shocks.

Priority guidance: define minimum liquidity and fiscal buffer targets that reflect country-specific exposure to disasters, revenue volatility, and financing constraints.
  • Set explicit fiscal buffer floors linked to disaster and macroeconomic shock exposure.
  • Protect liquidity needed for salaries, essential services, and emergency response.
  • Integrate disaster-risk financing into medium-term fiscal planning.

Policy focus

Build buffers before shocks occur, rather than relying only on emergency financing after disasters.

Operational angle

Link fiscal buffers to annual budgets, disaster-risk financing instruments, and medium-term fiscal frameworks.

Why it matters

Stronger buffers help Pacific SIDS absorb shocks while protecting vulnerable households and essential public services.

Together, we are building stronger fiscal resilience in the Pacific — sharing knowledge, empowering SIDS, and preparing for the next shock with solidarity and innovation.
